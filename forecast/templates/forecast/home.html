Autumn

In autumn, or fall, temperatures start to drop resulting in plants growing dormant. Some animals start to prepare themselves for the winter that lies ahead by storing food and traveling to warmer areas. Many cultures celebrate the season due the harvests that they receive, for instance Thanksgiving for example is a historical commemoration but there is actually a link between this and the season, giving praise to what was given.

Spring

In spring the seeds take root and vegetation begins to blossom. The weather is warmer resulting in animals waking or return from warmer climates, often with young offspring. The increased rainfall as well as the melting snow from the previous season provides the chance of flooding in water ways.

Summer

In summer temperatures start to increase and rise to the hottest of the year. Often times droughts and heat waves occur if the temp spikes too high, causing danger to people, animals and plants. One such scenario for example in summer of 2003, the extreme temperatures claimed more than 30,000 lives, according to Encyclopedia Britannica. Forest fires may become more frequent while rainfall may increase in some areas, as well.
